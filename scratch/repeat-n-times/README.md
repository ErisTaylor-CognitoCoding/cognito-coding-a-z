# Repeat N Times — Counted Loops

> **Do something a specific number of times, then stop.**

---

## The analogy

"Do 10 star jumps." Not "do star jumps forever" — exactly 10, then stop.

`forever` never ends. `repeat 10` counts to 10 and then carries on with whatever comes next. You're in control of how many times.

---

## The blocks

```scratchblocks
when green flag clicked
repeat (10)
    move (20) steps
    turn (36) degrees
end
say [Done!]
```

The sprite draws a circle (10 steps × 36° = 360°), then says "Done!" — the `say` block only runs after the loop finishes.

---

## Why it matters

In Python this is `for i in range(10):`. In most languages, counted loops have a start, an end, and a step. `repeat N` is the simplest form — just a count. Understanding the difference between "repeat 10" and "forever" is the same as understanding `for` vs `while True`.
